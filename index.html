🌡 August Weather in Melbourne

🌡Temperature: 8–15°C. Cold mornings, mild afternoons. Layer up.
🧥Clothing: Warm jackets, gloves for early mornings (Shrine sunrise), waterproof layer.
🌦Rain: August is rainy — pack compact umbrella. "Four seasons in one day" is literal in Melbourne.
🌊Great Ocean Road: Can be windy and cold on the cliffs — bring windbreakers for the kids.
🏖Beach: No swimming — too cold. But great for photos and walks.

💰 Money & Payments

💳Cards: Visa/Mastercard accepted everywhere. Tap-to-pay is ubiquitous in Melbourne.
💵Cash: Bring some AUD — smaller cafes, markets. ATMs widely available.
🏧Exchange: Do not exchange at airport — use Wise card or withdraw from ANZ/CBA ATMs.
🎫myki card: Melbourne's transit card — buy at airport 7-Eleven. Top up $40 each for 7 days.
💰Tipping: Not mandatory but 10% appreciated at sit-down restaurants.

🍽 Food & Dining

☕Coffee culture: Melbourne takes flat whites seriously. Try Patricia, Seven Seeds, ST Ali.
🥩Must-eat: Burger at Huxtaburger, dim sum at Flower Drum, pastries at Tivoli Road Bakery.
🌏Diverse dining: Melbourne's multicultural food scene is world-class — explore Chinatown on Little Bourke St.
🛒Breakfast at home: Stock the apartment kitchen — saves ~$50–80 AUD/day for 6 people vs cafe breakfasts every day.
🍫Souvenirs: Haigh's Chocolates, Tim Tams, Vegemite, Bundaberg ginger beer.

👨‍👩‍👧 Travelling with Kids

🎡Luna Park is great for kids — vintage rides, heritage park atmosphere.
🐨Healesville Sanctuary is the highlight for kids — hold a koala (check if allowed in 2026).
🚂Puffing Billy is universally loved — dangling legs from open carriage windows is the experience.
🦘Melbourne Zoo in Royal Park — good half-day option. Kangaroos, lions, butterflies.
🌿Fitzroy Gardens has a kids' fairy tree sculpture trail and miniature Tudor village.

🌐 Connectivity & SIM

📱Best SIM: Optus or Telstra prepaid at airport arrivals. ~$30 for 40GB/30 days.
📶Coverage: Telstra has best rural coverage for GOR and Dandenong Ranges. Worth the extra $5.
🗺Offline maps: Download Google Maps offline for Melbourne region before you land — saves data.
🔌Power: Australia uses Type I plugs (3-flat-pin). Buy adaptors in BKK or at MEL airport arrivals.
⚡Voltage: 230V / 50Hz — same as Thailand (230V) — most devices are fine, check labels.

🏥 Health & Safety

🏥Travel insurance: Get comprehensive cover for 6 pax — includes rental car excess (~$5,000 AUD).
💊Pharmacy: Chemist Warehouse everywhere — affordable cold/flu meds, sunscreen.
☀️Sun in winter: UV index can still be moderate in August — pack SPF 30+.
🚑Emergency: Call 000 for ambulance/police/fire in Australia.
🐍Wildlife: Stick to paths in Dandenong Ranges — snakes active in mild winter. No approaching.
